# UEOT Method

## Standard Analytical Frame

Every domain research pass should answer the following in order:

1. What is the object in this domain?
2. What candidate `Omega`-loops maintain its persistence?
3. How are `Pi` and `Phi` instantiated in domain language?
4. What are the object's `GOD` and `GOA`?
5. What lifecycle phases can be identified?
6. What multiscale or RG-style structure is relevant?
7. What external theories support, challenge, or compete with the UEOT framing?
8. What testable claims or concrete research actions follow?

## Required Output Shapes

### Research Memo

Each memo must contain:

- question
- constitutional primitives used
- domain object framing
- `Omega`-loop analysis
- `Pi/Phi` tradeoff analysis
- `GOD/GOA` derivation
- lifecycle interpretation
- external literature and objections
- at least 2 cross-domain links
- research consequences

### Cross-Review

Each review must identify:

- the claim under review
- the relation type: support, tension, bridge, critique, or dependency
- what the target domain must respond to

### Architect Resolution

Each resolution must identify:

- what was decided
- what remains unresolved
- follow-up work by domain
- what becomes canonical versus provisional

## Evidence Discipline

- The UEOT paper is the constitutional baseline.
- External literature is used for validation, stress testing, translation into
  domain language, and empirical benchmarking.
- Unsupported grand claims remain provisional.
- Analogies do not count as validation.

## Monograph Discipline

Book chapter prose may only be promoted to canonical draft status when:

- supporting memos exist
- counterarguments have been recorded
- architect review has occurred
- unresolved gaps are named explicitly
