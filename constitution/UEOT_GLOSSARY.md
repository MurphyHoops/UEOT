# UEOT Glossary

## Core Terms

### Omega-loop
A self-maintaining feedback loop whose continued operation regenerates the
pattern that constitutes an object's persistent identity.

### Object
A persistently existing entity understood as a maintained process rather than a
merely static thing.

### Pi
The physical efficiency, economy, payoff, or viability drive operating on an
object.

### Phi
The informational drive toward uncertainty reduction, model refinement,
adaptive complexity, or better internal ordering.

### GOD
Global Optimal Direction. The instantaneous or local direction of change that
best advances the object toward its longer-run optimum.

### GOA
Global Optimal Attractor. The longer-run optimal state or attractor implied by
the object's coupled dynamics.

### Loop Complexity (`L_c`)
The internal structural richness and nesting depth of an object's feedback
architecture.

### Loop Openness (`L_o`)
The degree to which an object's feedback loops are coupled to external flows of
matter, energy, or information.

### Type I Object
A relatively closed, highly stabilized object whose persistence depends mainly
on internal regulatory closure.

### Type II Object
A more open, adaptive, environmentally coupled object whose persistence depends
on active exchange and multiscale closure.

### Dynamical Mode
The qualitative temporal behavior of an object: stable, cyclic, or
transient/chaotic.

### RG Flow
A multiscale drift of object organization across emergence, optimization, and
decay, or across scale-dependent effective descriptions.

### Research Memo
The primary unit of domain research output. A memo must state the question,
constitutional primitives used, cross-domain links, and testable or actionable
consequences.

### Cross-Review
A structured critique from one domain to another intended to expose tension,
missing bridges, or integration opportunities.

### Architect Resolution
A binding written ruling from the chief architect that accepts, rejects,
splits, or defers claims and sets next-step constraints.
