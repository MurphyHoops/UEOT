# Socioeconomic Baseline

## Core Restatement

UEOT reads markets and institutions as multiscale feedback organizations whose
order and pathology depend on how well local optimization aligns with larger
system trajectories.

## Domain Objects and Candidate Omega-Loops

Candidate objects include markets, firms, institutional regimes, and larger
civilizational coordination structures. Their `Omega`-loops lie in repeated
capital, information, regulatory, and behavioral closures that preserve system
identity over time.

## Pi/Phi Translation

- `Pi`: short-term gain, allocative efficiency, material payoff
- `Phi`: risk recognition, information quality, resilience, institutional
  learning

## GOD / GOA

- `GOD`: local price, rule, and incentive directions that improve larger-scale
  coordination
- `GOA`: a more stable and efficient adaptive order

## Lifecycle Interpretation

Socioeconomic objects cycle through innovation, consolidation, overreach,
crisis, and reorganization. This is a natural arena for RG-style reading.

## Testable Consequence

UEOT should clarify when bubbles or crises reflect synchronized `Pi` dominance
over `Phi` and when instability is caused by boundary failure at higher levels.

## Cross-Domain Links

- Formal foundations must define what counts as a real social object rather
  than a descriptive aggregate.
- Meaning/philosophy must clarify how normative language relates to UEOT's
  naturalized teleology.
