# Formal Schema Baseline

## Core Restatement

UEOT cannot remain a serious research program if its primitives stay only
evocative. The formal foundations domain therefore treats `Omega`-loop,
`Pi/Phi`, `GOD`, and `GOA` as a minimal schema that must eventually admit
explicit mathematical representation.

## Domain Objects and Candidate Omega-Loops

The target object here is the **UEOT object form itself**. Its candidate
`Omega`-loop is the self-regenerating closure that keeps a pattern identifiable
across time despite local material turnover.

## Pi/Phi Translation

- `Pi`: viability, energetic economy, dynamic persistence efficiency
- `Phi`: uncertainty reduction, model stability, retained adaptive structure

## GOD / GOA

- `GOD`: the local direction that increases persistence under the coupled
  objective
- `GOA`: the attractor corresponding to stable, efficient, information-rich
  self-maintenance

## Lifecycle Interpretation

- emergence: candidate closure forms under high openness and instability
- maturation: closure stabilizes and filters noise more selectively
- decay: regulation weakens and loop integrity fails

## Testable Consequence

Any future formalization must distinguish genuine persistent closure from a
mere driven cycle. If no such distinction can be made, UEOT's ontology is too
weak.

## Cross-Domain Links

- Physics needs this schema to keep particle and gravity conjectures from
  floating free of the core.
- Mind science needs this schema to distinguish recurrent processing from
  constitutive self-maintaining closure.
