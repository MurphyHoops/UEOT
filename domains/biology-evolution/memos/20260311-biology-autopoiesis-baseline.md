# Biology Baseline

## Core Restatement

Biology gives UEOT its most intuitive arena: living systems already appear as
self-maintaining, adaptive, far-from-equilibrium organizations that match the
theory's process ontology.

## Domain Objects and Candidate Omega-Loops

Cells, organisms, developmental programs, and ecological adaptive structures
are candidate UEOT objects. Their `Omega`-loops are not merely oscillations but
the closure relations that preserve organization despite material turnover.

## Pi/Phi Translation

- `Pi`: metabolic economy, reproductive viability, survivability
- `Phi`: environmental learning, regulatory refinement, adaptive complexity

## GOD / GOA

- `GOD`: local adaptive drift toward viability-preserving refinement
- `GOA`: a stable or metastable adaptive regime consistent with persistence

## Lifecycle Interpretation

Biology fits UEOT lifecycle logic strongly: emergence, growth, maturation,
aging, and dissolution are not optional extras but constitutive phases.

## Testable Consequence

UEOT should outperform loose systems language by identifying when a biological
loop is genuinely constitutive of object persistence and when it is secondary.

## Cross-Domain Links

- Mind science depends on biology to ground higher-order cognitive loops in
  living regulatory structures.
- Socioeconomics can borrow the maturation / crisis rhythm, but only with care
  not to flatten biological specificity.
