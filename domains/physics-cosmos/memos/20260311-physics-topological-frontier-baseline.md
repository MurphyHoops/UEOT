# Physics Frontier Baseline

## Core Restatement

In physics, UEOT claims that stable physical entities may be understood as
topological persistence structures and that apparently incompatible formalisms
may reflect scale-dependent views of one underlying dynamical reality.

## Domain Objects and Candidate Omega-Loops

Candidate objects include particles, composite bound states, and large-scale
cosmological structures. The relevant `Omega`-loop language must remain
disciplined: it should refer to constitutive topological persistence, not any
pattern that merely looks cyclic.

## Pi/Phi Translation

- `Pi`: energetic economy, stability, dynamically efficient persistence
- `Phi`: informational ordering, structural constraint, reduced descriptional
  uncertainty

## GOD / GOA

- `GOD`: local drift toward more stable and law-consistent physical structure
- `GOA`: a stable attractor under the governing interaction landscape

## Lifecycle Interpretation

Emergent excitations form, stabilize, decay, and transform across scale. UEOT
must explain why some physical objects persist while others are only transient.

## Testable Consequence

Physical UEOT claims stand or fall on sharper resonance searches, decay
predictions, and RG-bridge proposals. If they never exceed metaphor, the
frontier fails.

## Cross-Domain Links

- Formal foundations must define what counts as a physically meaningful loop.
- Meaning/philosophy must keep the physics frontier from being rhetorically
  inflated into unjustified metaphysics.
